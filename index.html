About

Sohel Ahmed is a PhD researcher in Mathematics with research interests in biofluid mechanics, solute dispersion, and theoretical modelling of transport processes in complex biological flows. His work combines analytical methods, numerical simulations, and scientific computing to study fluid transport phenomena relevant to physiological and biomedical systems.

His research focuses on mathematical modelling of solute transport in Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluid flows, including micropolar and Casson fluid models. He is particularly interested in dispersion processes, reactive transport, and transport mechanisms in blood flow and related biological geometries.

Research Interests

Biofluid Mechanics

Mathematical and computational study of fluid flow in biological systems, especially blood flow and transport processes.

Solute Dispersion

Analysis of solute transport, convection, diffusion, and dispersion in complex flow environments.

Micropolar Fluid Flow

Modelling fluids with micro-rotational effects and studying their influence on velocity and concentration fields.

Reactive Transport

Study of transport phenomena involving wall reactions, reversible and irreversible reactions, and physiological applications.

Selected Projects

Solute Dispersion in Blood Flow
